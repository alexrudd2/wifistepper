{% mdicon led-off %}Issues & Troubleshoot

Issue Possible Cause Possible Solution
#1 Motor stalls or doesn't move when commanded. The voltage/current limit may be too low. Increase the KT Values (KT Hold, KT Run, KT Accel, KT Decel) to appropriate levels. Under current mode, typically at least 2 amps. Under voltage mode, at least 5%
#2 Motor get's too warm/hot after short amount of time. The voltage/current limit may be to high. Decrease the KT Values (KT Hold, KT Run, KT Accel, KT Decel) to appropriate levels. KT values too high may cause damage to the motor.
#3 Command Error flag is thrown. Communication errors between microcontoller and motor driver. Ensure the SPI pins (CLK, MOSI, MISO, CS) are not accidentally shorted or connected to.
#4 Over Current flag is thrown whenever motor is commanded You may be commanding too much current Lower the KT Values to appropriate levels. See issue #2.
#5 Under Voltage flag is thrown whenever motor is commanded. Your power supply may be entering CC mode and dropping the voltage. The power supply may be hitting the current limits. If the power supply is already at peak current, it may not be powerful enough for your current application. Try lowering the KT Values to command less voltage/current.
#6 Under Voltage flag is thrown whenever motor is commanded The voltage mode jumper may be soldered. The jumper on the bottom side if the board may be soldered over, bypassing the current sense resistor. While it is more efficient running in voltage mode, this jumper must be "open" (unsoldered) for current mode. Remove any residual solder bridging the jumper.
#7 Stall Detect warning is thrown when motor is spinning Motor is under stall or stall threshold is too low. Increase KT Values and ensure motor is not stalling. If issue persists, raise Stall Voltage threshold in Advanced Settings.
If the issue you have is not listed here, check wifistepper.com/troubleshoot.html
